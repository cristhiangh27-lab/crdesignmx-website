## Concept
Brisa Pavilion is an architectural proposal for a lightweight hospitality and contemplative pavilion located in Riviera Nayarit. The project is conceived as a structure open to the landscape, designed to provide shade, rest and gathering in a coastal setting where wind, natural light and ocean views define the spatial experience.

The pavilion operates as a transition between interior and exterior. Rather than closing itself off from its surroundings, it frames them through an open platform, free circulation and a roof that carries the project's formal identity.

## Roof and Atmosphere
The roof is the central architectural element: an organic radial geometry inspired by hypar surfaces, reinterpreted through a lightweight timber and metal structure.

Its undulating form creates height variation, cross ventilation and a sense of movement, recalling a sail, an open flower or a membrane tensioned by the sea breeze.

## Architectural Program
The program is organized as a flexible public or semi-private space within a hotel, tourism or recreational context.

- Circular platform for gathering, waiting, resting or contemplation.
- Central shaded area below the main roof, with enough height for free circulation and lingering.
- Open perimeter zones oriented toward the landscape and ocean views.
- Flexible area for light events, gastronomic experiences, cocktails or hospitality activations.
- Open 360-degree circulation without enclosed facades.
- Structural support points integrated into the pavilion's radial language.

## Structural System
The proposal balances formal expression with construction feasibility. Instead of using a monolithic concrete roof, the system is resolved through lighter components: steel structure, rigid perimeter edge and timber laminate or curved panel cladding.

The assembly is organized through a central mast or structural core, perimeter steel columns, radial beams, secondary ribs, diagonal tension members, a lower structural ring and a continuous metal edge that defines the undulating roof silhouette.
