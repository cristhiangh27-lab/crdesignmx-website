## Purpose
Helenia Atelier is an architectural proposal near Calzada de Taxqueña in Mexico City, conceived as a workspace, learning environment and training facility for women in vulnerable situations who want to enter the field of sewing and garment production.

The project frames the atelier as a form of support infrastructure: a place where architecture contains productive activity while also supporting learning, collaboration and economic autonomy.

## Concept
The name is inspired by one of the cities mentioned in the work of Italo Calvino, connecting the idea of an imagined city with a productive refuge and a space for social transformation.

Under this premise, the building is designed as a contemporary workshop where materiality, light and envelope create an identity tied directly to textile craft.

## CR Collective Scope
CR Collective participated in the structural facade proposal: a steel envelope with triangulated panels that evoke the movement, folds and tension of fabric.

This metal skin works as a second expressive structure. It protects the interior, filters daylight and gives the project an urban presence while preserving a direct relationship with the manual craft at its core.

## Collaboration
The proposal was developed in collaboration with architects Mario Alejandro Suárez Rodríguez and Diego López González, bringing architecture, structure, materiality and social narrative into a single formal gesture.
